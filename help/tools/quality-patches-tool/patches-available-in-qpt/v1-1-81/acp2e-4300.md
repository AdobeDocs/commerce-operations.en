# ACP2E-4300: Storefront catalog permissions not updating after Admin customer group change

The ACP2E-4300 patch fixes the issue where changing a customer group in the Admin does not update catalog permissions on the Storefront, causing the catalog and cart to reflect outdated permissions. This patch is available when the [[!DNL Quality Patches Tool (QPT)]](/help/tools/quality-patches-tool/quality-patches-tool-to-self-serve-quality-patches.md) 1.1.81 is installed. The patch ID is ACP2E-4300. Please note that this issue is scheduled to be fixed in Adobe Commerce 2.4.9.

## Affected products and versions

**The patch is created for Adobe Commerce version:**

* Adobe Commerce (all deployment methods) 2.4.7-p5

**Compatible with Adobe Commerce versions:**

* Adobe Commerce (all deployment methods) 2.4.5 - 2.4.8-p5

>[!NOTE]
>
>The patch might become applicable to other versions with new [!DNL Quality Patches Tool] releases. To check if the patch is compatible with your Adobe Commerce version, update the `magento/quality-patches` package to the latest version and check the compatibility on the [[!DNL Quality Patches Tool]: Search for patches page](https://experienceleague.adobe.com/tools/commerce-quality-patches/index.html). Use the patch ID as a search keyword to locate the patch.

## Issue

Changing a customer group in the Admin does not update catalog permissions on the Storefront, causing the catalog and cart to reflect outdated permissions.

<u>Steps to reproduce</u>:

1. In Admin, go to **[!UICONTROL Stores]** > **[!UICONTROL Settings]** > **[!UICONTROL Configuration]**. In the left panel, expand  **[!UICONTROL Catalog]** and choose  **[!UICONTROL Catalog]**. Expand **[!UICONTROL Category Permissions]**. Set **[!UICONTROL Enable]** to **[!UICONTROL Yes]**. Set **[!UICONTROL Allow Browsing Category]** to **[!UICONTROL Yes]**, for **[!UICONTROL Specified Customer Groups]**. Select **[!UICONTROL General]** in Customer Groups. 
1. Create a simple product and assign it to a category.
1. Go to **[!UICONTROL Catalog]** > **[!UICONTROL Categories]**, select the category, expand **[!UICONTROL Category Permissions]**, click **[!UICONTROL New Permission]**, choose the applicable **[!UICONTROL Website]** and **[!UICONTROL Customer Group]** as retailer, and set **[!UICONTROL Browsing Category]**, **[!UICONTROL Display Product Prices]**, and **[!UICONTROL Add to Cart]** to **[!UICONTROL Deny]**.
1. Create a customer account on the storefront with the default **[UICONTROL General]** customer group.
1. Add the product to the cart.
1. Go to **[!UICONTROL Customers]** > **[!UICONTROL All Customers]**. Select the customer, set the **[!UICONTROL Actions]** control to **[!UICONTROL Assign a Customer Group]**, choose **[!UICONTROL Retailer]**, and click **[!UICONTROL OK]** when prompted to confirm.
1. Refresh the cart or proceed to checkout on the storefront.

<u>Expected results</u>:

1. Restricted products should be removed from the cart when the customer proceeds to checkout.
1. Restricted products should not be available to add to the cart from the category page.

<u>Actual results</u>:

1. The product remains in the cart.
1. The customer can place an order for the product.
